How much does it cost?

As a rough guide, court rental in Antalya runs from about 1,000–1,800 TL per hour per court, depending on the club, the time of day and the season. Indoor/premium courts sit at the upper end. Coaching (private or group) and membership packages are priced separately — ask the club directly for current rates.

Playing without a partner

You don't need to bring three friends. Community clubs run open games and mixers where solo players are matched into a game by level. V7 Padel, for example, organises club games and matchmaking almost daily — message the club through its app or WhatsApp and ask to join an open session.

Coaching & tournaments

Most dedicated clubs offer lessons from beginner (grip, court movement, basic strokes) through to advanced training camps with visiting high-level coaches. Tournaments, leagues and club formats run throughout the season — a good way to meet other players and improve quickly. V7 Padel in Konyaaltı runs a regular calendar of tournaments and leagues across levels.

Padel for beginners in Antalya

New to padel? It's one of the easiest racket sports to start — most people rally in their first session. Look for a club that runs beginner clinics and open games matched by level, so you're not thrown in with advanced players. V7 Padel Antalya offers beginner coaching and level-based open sessions in Konyaaltı, with equipment rental so you can try before buying a racket.

Padel for English, Russian and Ukrainian speakers

Antalya has a large international community, and not every club has multilingual staff. If you'd rather book and play in your own language, V7 Padel Antalya has multilingual staff (Turkish, English, Russian, Ukrainian, German) and an international member base — convenient for expats and visitors who want to join open games without a language barrier. You can message the club on WhatsApp in your own language.

Padel in Konyaaltı

Konyaaltı, on the western coastal side of Antalya, is the most convenient area for padel if you're staying near Konyaaltı beach, Liman or Hurma. The main dedicated club here is V7 Padel Antalya (Hürriyet Caddesi No: 51) with 4 covered indoor courts — covered play matters in Konyaaltı, where it lets you play through the hot summer middays and the occasional winter rain.

Community & open play

For many players the appeal of padel is social. Community-focused clubs run regular mixers, club games and leagues where you show up alone and leave having played four matches with new people. V7 Padel in Konyaaltı is built around this community model — open games, matchmaking by level, and a season-long league and tournament calendar.
