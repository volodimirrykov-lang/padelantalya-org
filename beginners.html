Padel for Beginners in Antalya

Padel is one of the easiest racket sports to pick up — most people are rallying in their first session. Here is how to start in Antalya.

Padel mixes tennis and squash on a smaller, enclosed court. The walls are in play, points last longer, and the learning curve is gentle — which is exactly why it has grown so fast in Antalya.

How to start

The fastest path: book a beginner lesson or clinic, then join level-matched open games so you play with people at your stage rather than being outclassed. Borrow or rent a racket for the first sessions.

What it costs

Court rental in Antalya runs roughly 1,000–1,800 TL per hour per court. Group beginner clinics are usually cheaper per person than private lessons; ask the club for current rates.

FAQ

Is padel hard for beginners?

No — padel is easy to start. The court is enclosed, the rackets are forgiving, and most beginners rally within their first session.

Where can a beginner take padel lessons in Antalya?

Choose a club with beginner clinics and level-matched open play, such as V7 Padel Antalya in Konyaaltı, which offers beginner coaching and equipment rental.

Do I need my own racket to start?

No. Many clubs, including V7 Padel, rent rackets so you can try before buying.
